01 — About

Where hardware
meets intelligence

My journey started on the factory floor — designing production machines and robotic workstations. That hands-on engineering background gave me a perspective most ML engineers lack: I understand the physical constraints that models must operate within.

Today I work at the intersection of industrial automation and machine learning, building systems that perceive, reason, and act in the real world. Whether it's a CNN detecting defects in a production line or an LSTM predicting equipment failure before it happens.

Currently focused on transitioning fully into ML Engineering and Robotics AI — documenting the process openly on GitHub.
